History

The Solar System formed a long time ago.

In fact, it all began around 4.5 billion years ago...

Originally, the Solar System was a simple cloud of interstellar dust and gas.

The cloud collapsed into a solar nebular- a disk consisting of swirling gas and dust from the cloud.

Gravity began to pull material in more and more material into the center until the pressure was so great that hydrogen atoms began to combine together to form helium, which is called nuclear fission. This released a tremendous amount of energy, forming the Sun.

More debris and dust began to gather into clumps orbiting around the Sun, forming planets, asteroids, and moons.

The Sun's gravity pulled the planets towards it, but they travelled fast enough that they remained in an orbit around the Sun.

Ta-daa! The Solar System is now fully formed, and from here each planet began to develop its structure further through meteor strikes and the formation of moons until we reached the modern day Solar System.
